About This Journal

Everyday Life

Everyday Life is a peer-reviewed interdisciplinary journal dedicated to exploring the relationships between public culture, design, art, urban space, media, and everyday experience. Published biannually as a free open-access journal, it brings together academic research, visual culture, artistic practice, exhibition criticism, and experimental forms of inquiry that engage with the ordinary conditions of contemporary life.

The journal begins from a simple but increasingly urgent observation: everyday life is never neutral. Streets, interfaces, buildings, infrastructures, advertisements, domestic spaces, public images, objects, platforms, and forms of routine behaviour are all shaped by wider social, political, economic, and cultural forces. At the same time, everyday environments also remain sites of creativity, negotiation, conflict, memory, imagination, and public expression.

In recent years, public life has undergone profound transformations. Cities are increasingly mediated by digital systems, commercial images, logistical infrastructures, surveillance technologies, branding strategies, and forms of algorithmic governance. Public space has become simultaneously more connected and more fragmented; more visible and more controlled. Design now extends far beyond objects and aesthetics into the organisation of attention, behaviour, mobility, communication, and social relations themselves. Yet despite these changes, everyday life often remains overlooked precisely because it appears ordinary.

Everyday Life seeks to examine these conditions critically and creatively. The journal is interested not only in how contemporary environments are designed and governed, but also in how they are experienced, interpreted, appropriated, resisted, documented, and reimagined through artistic, spatial, and cultural practices.

Positioned across art and design studies, urban studies, architecture, visual culture, media theory, cultural geography, anthropology, sociology, and curatorial practice, the journal encourages dialogue between scholars, designers, artists, architects, filmmakers, curators, writers, and independent researchers. We are particularly interested in interdisciplinary work that crosses conventional boundaries between theory and practice, research and criticism, academic inquiry and cultural production.

Operating as a free open-access publication, all content in this journal is freely accessible online without subscription fees or article processing charges (APCs).

Types of Contributions

Research Articles
Peer-reviewed scholarly articles offering a clear academic contribution to the current issue theme through theoretical, historical, methodological, empirical, interdisciplinary, or practice-informed research.

Practice Review Articles
Critical reflections by artists, designers, architects, curators, filmmakers, photographers, cultural workers, and practice-based researchers on exhibitions, projects, visual research, spatial interventions, or creative practice.

Conversations and Interviews
Dialogues with artists, designers, architects, curators, researchers, and cultural practitioners.

Field Notes
Short observational or reflective texts engaging with everyday environments, public culture, spatial practices, material conditions, or situated experiences.

Open Access Statement
