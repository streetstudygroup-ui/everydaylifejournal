About This Journal

Everyday Life

Everyday Life is a peer-reviewed interdisciplinary journal dedicated to exploring the relationships between public culture, design, art, urban space, media, and everyday experience. Published biannually as a free open-access journal, it brings together academic research, visual culture, artistic practice, exhibition criticism, and experimental forms of inquiry that engage with the ordinary conditions of contemporary life.

The journal begins from a simple but increasingly urgent observation: everyday life is never neutral. Streets, interfaces, buildings, infrastructures, advertisements, domestic spaces, public images, objects, platforms, and forms of routine behaviour are all shaped by wider social, political, economic, and cultural forces.

At the same time, everyday environments also remain sites of creativity, negotiation, conflict, memory, imagination, and public expression.
